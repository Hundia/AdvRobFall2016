USB Data Link Traffic Manager

The Drones laboratory at Rabin building at Haifa university has an OptiTrack Tracker camera's set up that is used to determine a drone position in space. The position acquisition is transmitted over a UDP socket at a very high rate, and after processing at the base station the drones position are continuously updated. This workflow is done at extremely high rate, and the updates are going from the base station to an Arduino using a USB Connection. After experiencing connectivity issues with the drones, it has been determines that a possible latency issues is caused by the USB bottleneck that was used to control 8 drones simultaneously using one USB physical entry. To balance load the usb bus, I developed a usb manager that has a large TX buffer that can buffer up Tx messages to the drones without overloading the usb driver and at the same time not dropping messages toward the drones

Game Of Drones

First place project at CRI Hackaton 2016..! Augmented reality, drones, games. The ultimate combination. In a short period of time, With 3 partners we have managed to build and develop an augmented reality Arial combat game where the player wears a VR Helmet, that receives its live video feed from the drone, and plays against augmented adversaries in the real world. Credits to the Shwang! team for there amazing work bringing there Shwang gear (VR Goggles and Bluetooth remote) and customizing it to match the project needs at no time.

Billboard Flight

Research conducted with Daniel Jeyres revolving around lift commercial billboard signs in malls with a simple, safe for people, cheap affordable quad copters. During the research we tested different type of fabrics and materials. Also, we tackled communication issues revolving controlling several drones for long period of times while they overlap each other in the trackers line of view. The research is intended to pilot run in Grand Kenyon Mall in Haifa to show commercial advertisements to pedestrians in the mall.

  • Developers: Daniel Jeyres, Eli Hundia
